About Me

Hello! I'm Tasneem Fathima, a passionate second-year BSc Bioinformatics student at Garden City University. My fascination lies at the intersection of biology and computation, with a keen interest in genomic data analysis and computational biology. I am committed to leveraging my skills in Python and R to develop impactful solutions for complex biological challenges.

My ultimate aspiration is to contribute to a research team focused on vaccine design or personalized medicine, where my work can have a direct, positive impact on global health. I am a dedicated problem-solver, a collaborative team player, and I am always eager to learn and take on new challenges.

Projects

Exploration of the "Dark Genome"

A literature-based project exploring the functions of non-coding DNA. I conducted an extensive review of research papers from Pubmed and other academic sources to understand the latest findings on its role in genetic regulation and disease.

Bioinformatics in Modern Healthcare

Researched and compiled a report on the current applications of bioinformatics in clinical settings, including personalized medicine and drug discovery. I analyzed data from Pubmed and various bioinformatics publications to highlight key advancements and future trends.

CRISPR-Cas9: A Gene-Editing Revolution

An academic project focused on the mechanism and therapeutic potential of the CRISPR-Cas9 system. I utilized Pubmed to gather information on recent breakthroughs and ethical considerations surrounding gene-editing technology.
